# MeshMarket — Child Safety Standards

**App:** MeshMarket (`com.uniktek.meshmarket`)
**Developer:** Uniktek
**Last updated:** 2026-04-10

This page is the published Child Safety Standards for **MeshMarket**, a
peer-to-peer Bluetooth-mesh marketplace and messaging app published on Google
Play by **Uniktek**. It is provided in compliance with Google Play's Child
Safety Standards policy for social and dating apps.

## 1. Zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE)

MeshMarket and Uniktek have **zero tolerance** for child sexual abuse and
exploitation (CSAE), including but not limited to child sexual abuse material
(CSAM), grooming, sexual extortion of minors, trafficking of minors, and any
content or conduct that sexualizes, endangers, or exploits children.

The following are **strictly prohibited** on MeshMarket:

- Creating, uploading, transmitting, soliciting, sharing, requesting, trading,
  storing, advertising, or linking to child sexual abuse material (CSAM) of any
  kind.
- Sexualized depictions of minors, including computer-generated, drawn,
  animated, or AI-generated imagery.
- Grooming, sexual solicitation of minors, or any contact with minors for
  sexual purposes.
- Sextortion, coercion, or blackmail involving minors.
- Trafficking, smuggling, or commercial sexual exploitation of minors.
- Marketplace listings that offer, solicit, or facilitate any of the above.

Anyone found engaging in this conduct will be permanently removed from the
service, their cryptographic identity blocked, and — where lawful and possible —
reported to the appropriate authorities (see Section 4).

## 2. Compliance with applicable laws

Uniktek complies with all applicable child safety laws and regulations in the
jurisdictions where MeshMarket is distributed, including but not limited to:

- **United States:** 18 U.S.C. §§ 2251–2258A (federal child sexual exploitation
  statutes) and the reporting obligations of 18 U.S.C. § 2258A to the National
  Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC).
- **European Union:** Regulation (EU) 2021/1232 and the Digital Services Act
  (Regulation (EU) 2022/2065).
- **United Kingdom:** the Online Safety Act 2023.
- **Australia:** the Online Safety Act 2021.
- All other applicable national and local child protection laws where
  MeshMarket users reside.

## 3. In-app user feedback and reporting mechanism

MeshMarket provides an **in-app mechanism** that allows any user to report
abusive content, abusive users, or CSAE concerns:

- **Block user:** Long-press any peer in a chat or marketplace listing and
  select **"Block"** to immediately stop receiving any messages, listings, or
  contact from that cryptographic identity.
- **Report to developer:** Users can report CSAE, CSAM, or any child-safety
  concern directly to Uniktek by email at the address listed in Section 4.
  Reports can be sent from inside the app via the **About → Report a safety
  concern** link, which opens the user's email client pre-addressed to the
  child-safety contact.
- **What to include in a report:** the cryptographic peer ID (or nickname) of
  the offending user, an approximate date/time, the geohash channel (if any),
  and — only if you can do so safely and lawfully — a description of the
  content. Do **not** attach or forward CSAM; describe it instead.

Because MeshMarket is a decentralized peer-to-peer mesh, content is exchanged
directly between user devices and is end-to-end encrypted; Uniktek does not
operate servers that store user messages. Reports allow Uniktek to take action
on the application layer (such as blocklisting identities and shipping updates
that mitigate abuse) and to escalate to law enforcement where appropriate.

## 4. Child-safety point of contact

For all child-safety matters, including CSAE/CSAM reports, regulator inquiries,
and law-enforcement requests, contact Uniktek's designated child-safety point
of contact:

- **Name:** Uniktek Child Safety Team
- **Email:** **support@uniktek.tech**
- **Response target:** within 5 business days; urgent reports involving
  imminent harm to a child are prioritized.

## 5. How CSAM is addressed

When Uniktek receives a credible report of CSAM or CSAE involving MeshMarket:

1. **Triage:** the report is reviewed by the Child Safety Team within the
   response target above.
2. **Action on identity:** the offending cryptographic identity is added to
   Uniktek's distributed blocklist and pushed to clients in the next update,
   preventing the identity from interacting with other MeshMarket users.
3. **Reporting to authorities:** suspected CSAM is reported to the **National
   Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline** at
   <https://report.cybertip.org> and, where required, to local law enforcement
   in the relevant jurisdiction.
4. **Cooperation:** Uniktek cooperates with valid legal process from law
   enforcement and child-protection agencies to the extent technically
   possible, given MeshMarket's decentralized, end-to-end-encrypted design.
5. **Preservation:** any evidence lawfully in Uniktek's possession is
   preserved in line with applicable legal requirements.

Members of the public who encounter CSAM anywhere online — not only on
MeshMarket — are encouraged to report it directly to NCMEC's CyberTipline
(US/global) at <https://report.cybertip.org>, to the Internet Watch Foundation
at <https://report.iwf.org.uk>, or to their local police.

## 6. Age requirements

MeshMarket is **not directed to children under 13** (or the equivalent minimum
age in the user's jurisdiction, such as 16 in parts of the EEA). Users below
the applicable minimum age are not permitted to use the app.

## 7. Updates to these standards

Uniktek may update these standards to reflect changes in the law, in the app,
or in our safety practices. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page
will reflect the most recent revision.
